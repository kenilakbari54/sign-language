Sign Language


Sign language is a form of communication that uses visual cues such as speech, gestures, and body movements to convey meaning. Sign language is very useful for people who cannot hear or speak. Language recognition is the translation of these gestures into words or forms of existing languages. “Therefore, converting sign language into a word through algorithms or models could help bridge the gap between people with hearing or speech impairments and the rest of the world. Vision-based recognition is a field of current computer and machine learning. It is an area where many researchers work with the aim of making human-computer interaction (HCI) easier and more natural without the need for additional hardware,” as an effective method in human interaction. Therefore, the main goal of gesture recognition research is the ability to recognize human gestures and use them to send messages, etc. To create a system that can be used for to do this, vision-based gesture interfaces require fast and powerful hand detection and real-time gesture recognition. Gestures are a powerful form of human communication with many uses, and here we are talking about language recognition, a method of communication for deaf people.”
